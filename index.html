Located between the sea and the Nature Park Vransko Lake

Drage is located in the central part of the Croatian Adriatic and is situated on the isthmus between Vransko Lake and the Adriatic Sea, belonging to the peripheral area of Ravni Kotari. The most significant natural feature of Drage is its highly indented coastline and numerous bays— draga, both on the sea and lake shores, which is where the name of the settlement originates.

Drage was inhabited as far back as prehistoric times, as evidenced by the remains of the Liburnian settlement on the Čelinka hill above today's place (from the Iron Age). In the area of Drage, there are the remains of two more Liburnian forts, located on the peaks of Vela and Mala Kurela. However, they were not used for housing, but for defensive purposes.

Until about fifteen years ago, the inhabitants of Drage were mostly engaged in agriculture. However, nowadays, they are increasingly involved in the service industry. Tourism is highly developed in the area, as evidenced by the tourist resort Dolaška Draga, which has over 250 holiday homes. Near Drage is the Vransko Lake Nature Park, which stretches from Pakoštane to Prosika.
