THE ISLAND OF VRGADA

Vrgada

Blaž Jurišić, Croatian linguist, dialectologist, and author of the first Croatian dialectological dictionary.

Golden Vrgada


It was mentioned by Constantine VII Porphyrogenitus as early as the Middle Ages, as it was an important observation point for the Adriatic maritime route. Even today, the ruins of a medieval fortress still exist. This area is locally known as Gradina and is located on the hill of the same name above the settlement. The island is situated between Drage to the north, the island of Pašman to the northwest, the Kornati islands to the southwest, and Murter to the southeast.


Did you know? The island of Vrgada was named ‘Golden’ due to its beautiful beaches with special, golden sand that cannot be found on the mainland.

Enjoy the sun, sea and the sounds of crickets in the most beautiful sandy beach of the island, surrounded by greenery, and immersed in the crystal-blue sea.

Vrgada is a Dalmatian fishing island that has preserved the traditional ways of living without cars.

Take a walk down the streets that have been spared from the hustle and bustle of the town.

Escape the stresses of modern life and get back to nature on an island where time stands still.
